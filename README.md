
# Research Publication

## Predictive Embedded Analysis for Battery Degradation and Fire Risk Prevention in Electric Vehicles

This repository contains the research publication and conference proceedings related to the paper titled **“Predictive Embedded Analysis for Battery Degradation and Fire Risk Prevention in Electric Vehicles”**, presented at the **IEEE SB JIIT 1st Student Conference Research Forum 2026**.

The research focuses on improving the safety, reliability, and operational lifespan of electric vehicle battery systems through predictive embedded intelligence. Traditional Battery Management Systems (BMS) generally operate using fixed threshold values and reactive safety mechanisms that trigger only after abnormal thermal or electrical conditions occur. This work proposes a predictive embedded framework capable of identifying early signs of battery degradation and potential fire-risk conditions before critical failures arise.

The proposed system continuously monitors important battery parameters such as voltage, current, temperature, internal resistance, and thermal behavior using onboard embedded sensors and real-time analysis techniques. Embedded preprocessing, feature extraction, and predictive safety-oriented decision mechanisms are utilized to differentiate between normal operational variations and abnormal degradation patterns.

The work also explores preventive actions including adaptive charging control, temperature regulation, and intelligent load management to reduce electrochemical stress and enhance battery longevity. By performing local embedded analysis instead of cloud-dependent processing, the system aims to provide low-latency, reliable, and secure safety monitoring suitable for modern electric vehicle platforms.
